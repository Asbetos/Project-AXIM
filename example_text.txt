Boston College (Carroll) Business School Overview
The Carroll School of Management at Boston College (Carroll) offers these departments and concentrations: accounting, business analytics, consulting, finance, general management, leadership, manufacturing and technology management, marketing, management information systems, production/operations management, organizational behavior, portfolio management, supply chain management/logistics, quantitative analysis/statistics and operations research, tax, and technology. Its tuition is full-time: $65,080 per year (in-state); full-time: $65,080 per year (out-of-state); part-time: $2,128 per credit (in-state); part-time: $2,128 per credit (out-of-state); specialty master's: $2,128 per credit (in-state); and specialty master's: $2,128 per credit (out-of-state). At graduation, 71.20 percent of graduates of the full-time program are employed.

Full-time MBA program
The Boston College MBA Program welcomes applications from graduates of accredited colleges and universities. The Admissions Committee looks for evidence of sound scholarship and management potential. Work experience, academic excellence, leadership, and community involvement are also important factors in admissions decisions. Admissions information can be found at http://www.bc.edu/mba.
Specialty master's program
The Boston College MSA and MSF Programs welcome applications from graduates of accredited colleges and universities. The Admissions Committee looks for evidence of sound scholarship and management potential. Work experience, academic excellence, leadership, and community involvement are also important factors in admissions decisions. Admissions information can be found at http://www.bc.edu/msa and http://www.bc.edu/msf.
